Abstract

Despite the impressive performance in deep image colorization, existing methods still suffer from a critical problem called the color bleeding artifact. This issue, especially with the fine edges existing between adjoining objects, makes the colorization outputs less realistic, limiting their application to practical use. This paper proposes a novel user-interactive edge-enhancing colorization technique that takes a user-drawn scribble as input. This approach, which we call DEGAS, refines the representations of the backbone network by providing the edge-aware information in a target location. DEGAS encourages the model to generate a colorization output that is well-aligned with the semantic boundaries at the end. By leveraging automatically generated pseudo-scribbles as additional given condition during training, our approach can properly reflect the user's intent on where to correct in the edge-bleeding image. Quantitative and qualitative experimental results demonstrate that our proposed approach effectively improves the colorization of color-bleeding regions in various datasets.
